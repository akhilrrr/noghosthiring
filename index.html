Ghosting

/ˈɡəʊstɪŋ/

noun

  1. the sudden act of ending communication without notice or explanation.
    (“The recruiter stopped replying, a classic case of ghosting..”)

  2. in hiring: when a company requests applications, interviews, or tasks; then vanishes without response, leaving candidates in uncertainty and wasted effort.
